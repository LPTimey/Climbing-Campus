# Evaluation Statements: Climbing Campus

## Demographics

### Entity types

- 10 / 16 participants were **students** at THI
- 6 / 16 participants were **staff members** at THI

### Impairments

- 6 / 16 participants were impaired

## General campus experience

### General rating

- The average campus experience rating was 3 (neutral)

### Campus experience factors

1) Resting areas (11)
2) Clear spatial layout (10)
3) Lighting, digital infrastructure, ventilation options (9)
4) Good mobility (7)

### Suggested improvements

- x6: Building maintenance, paths, ventilation, outlets, less constructions
- x5: Accessibility measures, wayfinding, campus movement, barrier-free access
- x4: Study spaces, workstations, classroom technology
- x2: Plants, decoration, resting areas, social spaces

Normalized:

1) *x1:* plants, decoration, resting areas, social spaces
2) *x2:* study spaces, workstations, classroom technology
3) *x2.5:* accessibility, wayfinding, campus movement, barrier-free access
4) *x3:* buildings, maintenance, paths, ventilation, outlets, construction impacts

### Campus-experience-based feelings

- 50% of participants find their timetable clustered

2) The timetable supports my general motivation (AVG 3.2)
3) I experience regular relaxation periods (AVG 3.0)
4) I feel stressed by my timetable (AVG 2.8)

### Disadvantaged

- 100% of participants think that there are student wo are disadvantages

### Causes of disadvantages

1) Missing accessibility regarding impairments
2) Time-related disadvantages (e.g. commute)
3) Digital hurdles (WiFi, devices, plugs, etc.)
4) Unequal distribution of in-person classes
5) Culture-related disadvantages
6) Gender-specific disadvantages

### How to best tackle these disadvantages

1) Accessibility in mobility (elevators, ramps, tactile guiding systems, etc.)
2) Flexible timetable options (hybrid lectures, block weeks, buffers, etc.)
3) Courses & awareness for inclusion
4) Transparent resource distribution (slots, support, etc.)
